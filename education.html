Key Research Papers

Rough Volatility 2018

Volatility is Rough

Gatheral, Jaisson, Rosenbaum · Quantitative Finance

The foundational paper establishing that log-volatility behaves as a fractional Brownian motion with Hurst parameter H ≈ 0.1. Direct basis for the Volterra research paper.

Deep Hedging 2019

Deep Hedging

Buehler, Gonon, Teichmann, Wood · Mathematical Finance

Recasting derivative hedging as a reinforcement learning problem — the conceptual bridge between the RAMPA PPO agent and traditional derivatives risk management.

Transformers 2017

Attention Is All You Need

Vaswani et al. · NeurIPS

The architecture that redefined sequence modeling — studied for its positional encoding and multi-head attention mechanism in the context of financial time-series modeling.

Policy Gradient 2017

Proximal Policy Optimization Algorithms

Schulman, Wolski, Dhariwal, Radford, Klimov · OpenAI

The clipped surrogate objective and PPO's stability advantages over TRPO — directly implemented in RAMPA's portfolio allocation agent.

Neural Rough Vol 2019

Neural Network Calibration of rough Bergomi

Bayer, Horvath, Muguruza, Stemper, Tomas

Training neural networks to calibrate rough volatility models at inference speed — the theoretical basis for RAMPA's DNN Bergomi calibrator at millisecond latency.

Financial ML 2018

The Deflated Sharpe Ratio

Bailey & López de Prado · Journal of Portfolio Management

Correcting for backtest overfitting and multiple hypothesis testing in strategy evaluation — applied across all portfolio backtests to avoid inflated reported performance.

Hawkes Processes 1971

Spectra of Some Self-Exciting and Mutually Exciting Point Processes

Alan Hawkes · Biometrika

The original Hawkes process paper — the self-exciting structure that underlies both the HFT order flow model and the Volterra paper's jump-diffusion component.

Market Microstructure 2016

Market Microstructure Invariance

Kyle & Obizhaeva · Econometrica

Relating trade size, price impact, and market activity via invariance principles — context for the Hawkes-based HFT execution design and order flow analytics.
